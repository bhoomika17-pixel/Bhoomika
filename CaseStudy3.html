Guiding Scalable AI Experience Patterns Through Foundational Research

Uncovering gaps in trust, interaction patterns, and contribution workflows to inform AI design system strategy across builder teams.

Overview

Led foundational research to understand how builder teams were designing AI experiences. Identified gaps in guidance and trust signaling, directly informing design system strategy to enable consistent and scalable AI experiences.

Context

As AI adoption expanded, builder teams implemented diverse interaction models without shared guidance. This fragmentation created risks to user trust and the scalability of design system support. The goal was to evolve the system to support emerging models like multimodal and non-chat flows.

Role & Scope

UX Research Lead

Led multi-method foundational research, mapping decision-drivers, and aligning stakeholders on scalable AI interaction patterns.

Key Insights

01

Discoverability Drives Adoption

Teams applied guidance most consistently when it was embedded directly in their existing workflows rather than external documentation.

02

Patterns Build Trust

Critical guidance was needed for signaling AI involvement, processing states, and reducing cognitive load to make AI behavior trustworthy.

03

Beyond Chat-Based Models

Builder teams required flexible patterns for multimodal inputs and iterative generation, moving past traditional chat interfaces.

04

Contribution Barriers

Complex contribution processes led to duplicated efforts; clarifying these pathways was essential for ecosystem growth.
