UX RESEARCH LEAD

Improving Consistency and Effectiveness of Cross-Team Design Reviews at Scale

Foundational research to clarify roles, expectations, and insight flow in a high-frequency design review program.

Design Review Ecosystem

The Outcome

Informed program improvements to increase consistency, strengthen onboarding, and enable a more structured feedback loop across teams for high-frequency (2–4 daily) sessions.

Role & Scope

  • • Defined and led end-to-end research
  • • Identified gaps in roles and insight flow
  • • Delivered recommendations to Design System leadership

Context

The program was a required review for new services and high-priority launches. As adoption increased, differences in expectations and unclear roles created friction in how insights were applied across 2–4 daily sessions.

Research Approach

Designed a multi-method study including:

  • Stakeholder interviews to define learning goals
  • Contextual inquiry through live session observation
  • Focus groups with participating product teams
  • Semi-structured interviews with reviewers and ambassadors

Program Impact

Launch Gate Quality

Increased the effectiveness of reviews as a quality gate, ensuring teams could engage confidently and apply feedback reliably before launch.

Systemized Feedback

Established a structured mechanism for sharing recurring insights with the design system team, improving visibility into common patterns.

Key Insights

visibility_off

Unclear Expectations

Participants were less confident in applying feedback when goals, success criteria, and feedback usage were not established early in the process.

groups

Role Ambiguity

Confusion between reviewers, ambassadors, and facilitators led to hesitation and inconsistent engagement during high-frequency sessions.

sync_problem

Inconsistent Flow

While recurring themes surfaced, there was no structured way to capture or route insights to inform design system guidance and prioritization.

format_quote

"Program experiences scale successfully when expectations are explicit, roles are clearly defined, and insights flow efficiently between participants and platform teams."
