Foundational UX Research

AI-Assisted Interface Generation: Guiding Strategic Product Direction

Case Study Visual

The Outcome

Led 0→1 research for an AI interface tool, identifying workflow friction that risked adoption. By aligning the roadmap with builder behaviors, we reclaimed 480+ engineering hours and pivoted the MVP toward high-value features.

Role & Scope

Lead UX Researcher: Multi-phase strategy, concept validation with 12 experts, and a longitudinal study involving 48 participants across 4 cohorts.

Context

As builder teams faced friction with repetitive interface construction, leadership explored a prompt-driven AI tool. However, the team faced key uncertainties: Would prompt-based generation align with existing workflows, and would users trust the output? Without early validation, there was a high risk of investing in capabilities that would not be adopted.

480+

Engineering Hours Reclaimed

Identified misaligned feature directions (like direct code export) early, preventing unnecessary development effort and resource waste.

Pivot

Strategic MVP Realignment

Synthesized findings for Director-VP leadership to shift focus from "prompt-from-scratch" to visual starting points and rapid iteration workflows.

Key Insights

Learnability

Prompt Fatigue

Users struggled to construct effective prompts. This friction slowed effective use and highlighted the need for better system transparency.

Trust

The Transparency Gap

Unclear system behavior and unexplained errors reduced trust. Users preferred refining existing designs over generating new ones from scratch.

Integration

Workflow Friction

Even technically correct outputs failed if they didn't fit existing team frameworks. Integration was more valuable than raw generation.

Reflection

format_quote This study reinforced that the adoption of AI-powered tools depends less on technical capability and more on clear mental models and seamless workflow integration. Builders must understand how a system works before they can trust and rely on it.
